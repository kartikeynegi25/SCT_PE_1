🌊 Extreme Weather: This extra heat changes our weather patterns, leading to bigger storms, wilder floods, and longer droughts.

🧊 Melting Ice: Higher temperatures are melting glaciers and ice caps, which causes sea levels to rise and threatens coastal cities.

🐻 Real-Life Example: In the Arctic, the sea ice is melting so fast that polar bears are losing their hunting grounds and struggling to find food.

Instead of just following strict, pre-written code, AI systems look for patterns in massive amounts of data to make decisions, solve problems, and learn from experience.

1) Streaming Recommendations: Netflix and Spotify analyze your past choices to predict what you will enjoy next.

3) Virtual Assistants: Tools like Siri or ChatGPT use natural language processing to understand your questions and generate human-like answers.

As an engineer, it is crucial to understand that AI is not magic; it is advanced mathematics, statistics, and coding.

The Core Limitation:

AI lacks true understanding and consciousness. It operates entirely on statistical probabilities derived from historical data. Because it cannot think conceptually, an AI can confidently generate incorrect facts (called hallucinations) or inherit human biases present in its training data.
